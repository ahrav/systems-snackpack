# Round 1: contract before throughput

- Session: 10
- Date: 2026-07-20
- Finding: Similar bit-mixing APIs do not make hashes, checksums, CRCs, and
  PRNGs interchangeable. The failure contract determines the algorithm class.
- Artifact: Three exact CRC-32C implementations share update and finalization
  semantics. A separate CRC-32/ISO-HDLC implementation exposes polynomial
  mismatch.
- Cost boundary: The experiment compares a bytewise table loop with one
  dependent hardware CRC chain over a repeatedly warmed 4 KiB input. It does
  not measure folding CRC, disk or network I/O, or dispatch on every call.
  Launch-to-exit includes startup, but the experiment does not isolate its cost.
- Evidence boundary: Twelve fresh, order-balanced process pairs own replication.
  Inner iterations only amortize timing overhead. Linked-binary disassembly
  records generated instructions without assigning causality.
- Measured result: The dedicated-instruction chain's median speedup over the
  bytewise table was 54.830x on the Arm host (`dev-dsk-ahrav-2b`, `aarch64`)
  and 21.886x on the x86-64 host (`xlg`, `x86_64`). Both
  hosts matched every checked CRC and fragmentation case.
- Outcome: Verify the exact data-integrity contract before benchmarking an
  implementation. Treat security, reproducibility, and corruption detection as
  different selection problems.
