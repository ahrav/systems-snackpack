The pooled common ratio is not a stable overhead estimate. When common ran

The selective speedup also shifted from 159.235x when selective ran second to
198.562x when it ran first. The logs establish an order interaction, but they do
not identify cache, frequency, predictor, allocator, or host activity as its
cause. Four warm-up queries did not establish order-independent steady state.

## Boundary

The timer spans 160 queries after four warm-up queries. It includes posting
probes and exact verification for the index path, or exact matching over every
document for the scan path. It excludes process startup, data generation, index
construction, warm-up, candidate reporting, and output.

The interval covers sequential process variation in one session, on one CPU,
for one binary, corpus, and query set. The deterministic schedule can alias host
activity, frequency state, or carryover and may violate the interval's
independence assumption. It does not cover other builds, hosts, corpora, query
distributions, AMD CPUs, or x86-64 systems.
