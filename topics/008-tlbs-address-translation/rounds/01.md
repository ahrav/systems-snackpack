# Round 1: translation reach and mapping-change scope

- Session: 8
- Date: 2026-07-18
- Finding: Page tables are authoritative mappings; per-CPU TLB entries are
  derived replicas. Huge pages change translation reach. Mapping mutations add
  an invalidation and completion cost.
- Artifact: A Linux mapping probe verifies base pages against fully collapsed
  2 MiB anonymous THP, follows one dependent random page ring, and measures
  permission changes with one or 16 pinned readers.
- Evidence boundary: Fresh processes own replication. The reach timer excludes
  mapping setup and warmup. The permission timer includes the complete
  `mprotect` pair and cannot isolate one kernel invalidation mechanism.
- Outcome: Use live mapping evidence and process-level pairs before changing
  page size or attributing mapping-change latency to shootdowns.
